Global Cybersecurity Regulations to Know

Cybersecurity isn’t just about strong passwords and antivirus software—it’s also shaped by laws and regulations around the world. Different countries and regions have their own rules for protecting personal data, securing critical infrastructure, and ensuring businesses handle information responsibly. In this section, you’ll find an overview of key global regulations—from the EU’s GDPR and NIS2 directives, to U.S. privacy acts, China’s cybersecurity laws, and international standards like ISO 27001. Understanding these rules helps you stay compliant, protect your personal data, and make smarter choices when interacting online or running a business. Think of it as a global map of digital rules, giving you context and confidence in today’s connected world.

United Kingdom

  • UK GDPR – UK’s version of the EU GDPR, ensuring data protection and privacy.
  • Data Protection Act 2018 – Complements UK GDPR, providing additional data rights.
  • NIS Regulations – Implements the NIS2 Directive for essential services in the UK.

European Union

  • GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation) – Sets strict rules for data protection and privacy across the EU.
  • NIS2 Directive – Enhances cybersecurity for essential services and digital service providers.
  • ePrivacy Directive – Governs privacy in electronic communications.

United States

  • HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act) – Protects sensitive patient health information.
  • CISA (Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency guidelines) – Offers frameworks for businesses to secure critical infrastructure.
  • California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) – Gives residents rights over personal data collected by businesses.

Asia

  • China: Cybersecurity Law – Governs data storage, personal information, and network security for companies operating in China.
  • Japan: Act on the Protection of Personal Information (APPI) – Regulates how businesses collect and handle personal data.
  • Singapore: Cybersecurity Act – Establishes frameworks to protect critical information infrastructure.

Australia

  • Privacy Act 1988 – Governs the handling of personal information by Australian entities.
  • Australian Cyber Security Centre (ACSC) guidelines – Provides cybersecurity advice and resources for businesses and individuals.
  • Notifiable Data Breaches (NDB) scheme – Requires entities to notify individuals and the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) of eligible data breaches.

International Standards

  • ISO/IEC 27001 – Provides requirements for an information security management system (ISMS).
  • ISO/IEC 27018 – Focuses on protecting personal data in the cloud.
  • NIST Cybersecurity Framework – Offers a policy framework of computer security guidance for how private sector organizations can assess and improve their ability to prevent, detect, and respond to cyber attacks.
